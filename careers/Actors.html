Traits of an Actor

Did I hear you say anybody can act? Well, almost everybody could, but it comes pretty easy for some personality than others. An extrovert could, introverts too. But Actors most dominant personality preference are Intuition and Feeling; most importantly they are with a perceiving orientation. Now, letâ€™s talk about these dominant preferences and how it relates to actorsâ€™ work descriptions. Shall we?

Intuition: Actors mostly prefer Intuition as a way to take in information. They focus on what facts mean and how they fit together; they pay more attention to implications, possibilities, and relationships between ideas than to facts and details alone, this highlights their Feeling preference. Actors tend to jump around between ideas and tasks as they work and learn; they like solving problems and have a great persistence determination to learn new skills at almost every time, skills that will help them solve those problems.

Feeling: Actors are individuals who mostly prefer feeling as a way to make decisions and process information. They are more interested in what effect their movies, roles and character will have on people, because the ideas they express directly affects the people, and they are motivated by these same people, so they make subjective decisions and follow them through. They are often good at understanding and appreciating the values of others.

Perceiving: Due to their nature of work, they tend to become frustrated with rules, routines, and closure; of a fact, most movies are at odds with laws, could be the law of nature, law of the land or humanity, they defer judgments and focuses on exploring and seeking new information; embrace change. In addition, they are flexible, adaptable and spontaneous, because of the need to collaborate with other actors as part of an ensemble, and them being open to change (positive or negative) and to considerable variety in the workplace.
