Who is Customer Service Representative

Customer service representatives work with customers to resolve complaints, process orders, and provide information about an organization’s products and services. They answer questions or requests from customers or the public. They typically provide services by phone, but some also interact with customers face to face, by email or text, via live chat, and through social media.

What does Customer Service Representatives do?:

  • Listen to customers’ questions and concerns and provide answers or responses
  • Provide information about products and services
  • Take orders, calculate charges, and process billing or payments
  • Review customer accounts and make changes, if necessary
  • Handle returns or complaints
  • Record details of customer contacts and actions taken
  • Refer customers to supervisors or more experienced employees

Traits of Customer Service Representatives

They are Extroverts – individuals that learn and work best when able to share, discuss, and process information with others; with a Perceiving orientation. Their most dominant personality preference is Feeling; they prefer either Sensing or Intuition as a way to take in information, but thinking is not likely to be their way of making decisions and processing information.

Feeling allows them to work in an environment that provides support and encouragement, they are mostly interested in the effects on information on the people than in things or ideas themselves, they relate more with the people, so they are motivated by the people, and hence they must be flexible and adaptable.
