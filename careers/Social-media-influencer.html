who is a Social Media Influencer

A social media influencer is someone that uses social media platforms and websites to promote a product or service. It might also be to promote a course. Since social networking websites are virtual communities. Marketers tend to use it to reach their prospective customers. It is also easy in reaching their core prospects, as it connects consumers and audiences to businesses that share the same needs, wants, and values.

Social media also provides the platform for companies, service providers, and influencers to have personal interaction with their followers and prospective followers. This personal interaction can instill a feeling of loyalty into followers and potential customers.

Influencers have been the most effective users of this platforms. Companies and service providers that are on these platforms hire them to help influence people; convinced them on how what the companies provide will benefit them.

Influencers who has the skills required to successfully influence and sell, has the power to turn a random individual into a prospect. Influencers however have their niche; could what they passionate about, what they know more about, or what they think majority of their community connects with more. And choose the platform where their audience are mostly on. Most of them are on various platforms: Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, and so on.

Skills of Social media influencers

The following are skills that make working as a Social Media Influencer efficient and successful. There are soft skills - (transferable skills), which some will refer to as abilities, or personal attributes of Social Media Influencers, there is also the hard skills – Knowledge and expertise that is highly required of you as a professional Social Media Influencer.

Soft skills of Social media influencers

Sales skills. Social Media Influencers must be able to persuade consumers to do things accordingly. Sometimes they might need to persuade companies to take care of their customer’s special needs. – For them to efficiently do this, they must possess the following soft skills-set:

  • Persuasion — Persuading others to change their minds or behavior.
  • Negotiation — Bringing others together and trying to reconcile differences.
  • Complex Problem Solving — Identifying complex problems and reviewing related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions.
  • Copywriting skills: Writing text for the purpose of advertising or other forms of marketing.

Interpersonal skills. Influencers need good communication and interpersonal skills because much of their work involves dealing with people. – For them to efficiently do this, they must possess the following soft skills-set:

  • Reading Comprehension — Understanding written sentences and paragraphs in work related documents.
  • Graphic Design — using the power of visuals to reach more people.
  • Speaking — Talking to others to convey information effectively.
  • Active Learning — Understanding the implications of new information for both current and future problem-solving and decision-making.
  • Coordination — Adjusting actions in relation to others' actions.
  • Service Orientation — Actively looking for ways to help people.
  • Social Perceptiveness — Being aware of others' reactions and understanding why they react as they do.

Problem-solving skills. Social Media Influencers help consumers find solutions to their problems. They must be able to listen carefully to their followers’ needs and offer practical solutions. – For them to efficiently do this, they must possess the following soft skills-set:

  • Curiosity — immersing yourself in the world social media. Staying up-to-date with the latest development.
  • Critical Thinking — Using logic and reasoning to identify the strengths and weaknesses of alternative solutions, conclusions or approaches to problems.
  • Complex Problem Solving — Identifying complex problems and reviewing related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions.
  • Negotiation — Bringing others together and trying to reconcile differences.
  • Systems Analysis — Determining how a system should work and how changes in conditions, operations, and the environment will affect outcomes.

Public Speaking. Influence might need to talk to consumers via live videos or on stage; talking to their audience. – For them to efficiently do this, they must possess the following soft skills-set:

  • Self-confidence — Persuading others to change their minds or behavior requires confidence and composure.
  • Critical Thinking — Using logic and reasoning to identify the strengths and weaknesses of alternative solutions, conclusions or approaches to problems.
  • Negotiation — Bringing others together and trying to reconcile differences.
  • Judgment and Decision Making — Considering the relative costs and benefits of potential actions to choose the most appropriate one.
  • Complex Problem Solving — Identifying complex problems and reviewing related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions.
  • Presentation Skills — Using the power of visuals to reach more people.

At a minimum, you must be familiar with social media platforms. You should also be comfortable using networking tools, such as ones designed to post across several social media accounts.
